## VOICEOVER SCRIPT

---

[Visual: Black screen. Sub-bass hum fades in. Hold 3 seconds.]

[Visual: Slow fade into sodium vapor orange light bleeding through chain-link fence. Rust Orange (#9A4C22) on Dark Steel (#1E1E20). Empty lot. Night.]

**TYRONE (V.O.):**

Mama lost the house in '03.

[1.5 second pause]

Wasn't no warning. Just a letter. Then a lock on the door.

[Visual: Close-up of padlocked chain on a gate. Rust. Peeling paint. Sodium vapor light catches the metal.]

She moved south. Family down in Louisiana said they had room.

[1.5 second pause]

I stayed.

[2 second pause]

[Visual: Red Nose — P5 The Close. One amber eye, scar across left brow. Still. Watching. Rust light on copper coat.]

I was seventeen. Sleeping in a Cutlass Supreme with a pit bull and a stolen laptop.

[1.5 second pause]

Three years like that. Couches. Cars. Places nobody should close their eyes.

[Visual: Red Nose — P3 variant. Lying on cracked concrete beside a closed laptop. Head on paws. One eye open. Single sodium vapor light source casting long shadow.]

[1.5 second pause]

But that laptop changed something.

[Visual: Faint glow of a screen in a dark interior. Bone White (#E7E7E1) light on hands. No face visible.]

Library WiFi. Community center when they'd let me in. I started reading things I wasn't supposed to understand.

[1.5 second pause]

Tax codes. Lien laws. How credit actually works. How bail bonds make money. Who owns what in which neighborhood, and why.

[Visual: Text fragments fading in and out on Dark Steel — "LIEN LAWS" / "CREDIT SYSTEMS" / "PROPERTY RECORDS" — JetBrains Mono, Rust Orange (#9A4C22), telemetry style. Quick cuts.]

[1.5 second pause]

The same way some people hear music — I started hearing systems.

[2 second pause]

[Visual: Red Nose — P1 The Watch. Sitting upright, 3/4 angle, looking off-frame. Coveralls visible. Urban decay background. Composed.]

I tried to put people on. Ran workshops out of barbershops. Explained how to fight an eviction. How to read a contract before you sign your life away.

[1.5 second pause]

Half those nights ended wrong. TTP drama. Somebody owed somebody. Guns where there should've been questions.

[Visual: Hard cut to black. Hold 2 seconds.]

[Visual: Slow fade back in — Red Nose — P6 The Aftermath. Walking through empty urban street. Mid-stride. Sodium vapor streetlight casting long shadow behind him. Cracked pavement. Abandoned storefronts.]

But the dog stayed.

[1.5 second pause]

Through all of it. He stayed.

[2 second pause]

[Visual: Red Nose stops walking. Turns head slowly toward camera. One eye catches the rust orange light. Ears natural, folded back. Scar visible. Hold 3 seconds.]

I didn't get a record deal. Didn't get a scholarship. Didn't get saved by nobody.

[1.5 second pause]

*(volume drops)*

I just learned how things work. And I never forgot what it felt like not to know.

[2 second pause]

[Visual: Red Nose — P4 The Silhouette. Back to camera, sitting on rooftop edge. City lights below. Orange sodium vapor glow. Night. Fog. Hold 4 seconds.]

*(near whisper)*

That's The Aftermath. I already lived through the collapse.

[1.5 second pause]

That's why I see it coming for everybody else.

[Visual: Slow fade to Dark Steel (#1E1E20). Hold 3 seconds.]
[End card: Red Nose silhouette + "BOWTIE BULLIES" Anton condensed uppercase + subscribe CTA]

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Pacing**: Every [pause] is load-bearing. Do not compress them. The silence IS the story.
- **Volume drops** on the final two lines are critical — Tyrone gets quieter when it matters most. The listener leans in.
- **Red Nose** appears four times, each at a different emotional beat: witnessing (P5), enduring (P3), watching (P1/P6), and reflecting (P4). He is not decoration. He is the only one who was there.
- **"TTP drama"** is spoken casually, no emphasis — it's context, not a flex. Trap Turf Posse is mentioned the way someone mentions weather. It was just the environment.
- **The Cutlass Supreme** is specific and intentional — it's a car you sleep in, not a car you show off. It places Tyrone in a time and a tax bracket.
- **No face is ever shown.** Hands on the laptop. That's as close as we get to a human body.
- **Final line sits alone.** No music sting. No button. Just the words, then darkness, then the end card. Let it breathe.
