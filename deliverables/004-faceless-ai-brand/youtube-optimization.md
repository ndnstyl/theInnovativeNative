### Description Writing Rules

1. **First 150 characters are everything.** This is what shows in search results and browse before "Show More." Make it a hook, not a summary.
2. **Primary keyword in the first line.** YouTube indexes the description for search. The first 2-3 lines carry the most weight.
3. **Timestamps are mandatory.** YouTube uses them for chapters (which appear in search results) and for suggested clips. Every list item gets a timestamp.
4. **Affiliate links section:** Maximum 3 products per video. One is ideal. Include FTC disclosure.
5. **Keyword paragraph:** 2-3 natural sentences at the bottom. Include the primary keyword, 2-3 secondary keywords, and the year. This helps YouTube understand what the video is about without being spammy.
6. **Three hashtags maximum.** YouTube shows the first 3 above the title. More than 3 and YouTube ignores all of them. Use: #[PillarTag] + #[TopicSpecific] + #BowTieBullies (e.g., #FinancialSurvival #DebtFree #BowTieBullies or #FoodSecurity #UrbanGardening #BowTieBullies)
7. **No links to competitors.** No links to other channels. No links to other platforms except your own IG.
8. **Related video links:** Once you have 5+ videos, add a "Watch Next" section above the keyword paragraph with links to 2-3 related videos from your own channel.

### Title Rules

1. **50-70 characters ideal.** YouTube truncates at ~70 characters in search results and ~100 in browse. Front-load the hook.
2. **Primary keyword in first 40 characters.** YouTube weighs the beginning of the title most heavily for search.
3. **Never use ALL CAPS for the entire title.** One or two capitalized words for emphasis only if needed. The thumbnail carries the visual weight.
4. **Numbers outperform.** "5 Ways" > "Several Ways." Odd numbers slightly outperform even.
5. **Never use clickbait that the video doesn't deliver.** If the title says "5 Things," the video must contain exactly 5 things.
6. **Pipe character (|) or dash (—) to separate concepts.** Example: "5 Algorithms Running Your Life | The AI Bias Nobody Talks About"
7. **Year is optional.** Include if the content is time-sensitive: "The 2026 AI Timeline Nobody's Ready For"

### A/B Testing Protocol

YouTube now supports A/B thumbnail testing natively. For titles:

1. **Publish with your primary title** (best guess based on formulas above)
2. **After 48 hours:** Check CTR in YouTube Studio
   - CTR > 6%: Keep the title. It's working.
   - CTR 4-6%: Consider swapping. Test an alternative formula.
   - CTR < 4%: Swap the title immediately. Try a different formula entirely.
3. **When swapping:** Change only the title OR the thumbnail, never both at once. You need to isolate which variable matters.
4. **Log every swap** in Airtable (original title, new title, CTR before, CTR after, date of change) so you build a dataset of what works.

## 4. WATCH TIME RETENTION STRATEGY

Average View Duration (AVD) is the single most important metric for YouTube's algorithm. A 10-minute video with 60% AVD will outperform a 10-minute video with 30% AVD every time, regardless of total views.

### Retention Anchor Map

Place a retention anchor at each of these timestamps. A retention anchor is a moment designed to prevent the viewer from clicking away.

| Timestamp | Anchor Type | What To Do | Why |
|-----------|------------|-----------|-----|
| **0:00-0:05** | First Frame Hook | Text overlay visible in browse + VO starts immediately (no intro music, no logo, no "hey guys") | 20-30% of viewers leave in the first 5 seconds. You get ONE shot. |
| **0:30** | Context Lock | Deliver the "why now" — a news item, a stat, a story that anchors the video to THIS moment | Viewers who pass 0:30 are 3x more likely to finish the video |
| **2:00** | List Start / Promise Delivery | Begin delivering on the title's promise. If title says "5 Things," item #1 starts here. | Viewers are checking if you deliver. Start delivering early. |
| **3:00** | Pattern Interrupt #1 | Change visual rhythm — switch from talking-head equivalent to a stat callout, a Red Nose reaction shot, or a hard cut to new visual context | 3 minutes is the first "boredom checkpoint" |
| **5:00** | Mid-Point Re-engagement | Verbal open loop: "But the one nobody talks about is coming up." or "Number [X] is the one that changed how I think about this." | 50% of remaining viewers consider leaving at the midpoint |
| **7:00** | Pattern Interrupt #2 | Dramatic visual shift — letterbox format, Red Nose silhouette, or sudden silence (1.5-2 seconds of nothing) | Break the rhythm before the viewer's attention drift does it for them |
| **8:30** | "The Deeper Layer" Hook | Verbal signal: "But here's the part nobody's talking about." Visual: slow, contemplative, different energy from the list section | This is where casual viewers leave and your core audience leans in |
| **9:00+** | Affiliate/CTA Window | Natural product mention, then the close. Keep this tight — 60-90 seconds maximum. | Do NOT let the video drag after the main value is delivered |
| **Last 30s** | End Screen Zone | Slow fade, Red Nose sits still, "Pay attention." End screen card appears. | YouTube's end screen requires the last 20 seconds. Plan for it. |

### Pattern Interrupt Techniques

Pattern interrupts prevent the viewer's brain from auto-piloting (which leads to clicking away). Use 4-6 per long-form video.

| Technique | How It Works | When To Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Visual Scale Shift** | Cut from wide shot to extreme close-up (or vice versa) | Between list items, before a key stat |
| **Silence Drop** | VO stops. Music dips to -40dB. Hold 1.5-2 seconds. Then resume. | Before the most important sentence in the video |
| **Format Break** | Switch from narrative to stat overlay (giant number fills frame) or quote card | When presenting data, before "The Deeper Layer" |
| **Direct Address** | "And this is the part I need you to actually hear." / "Pay attention to this next one." | Once per video maximum. Overuse kills the effect. |
| **Red Nose Reaction** | Cut to Red Nose lifting his head, ears forward — he noticed something | When introducing a threat, a hidden truth, a system that's already running |
| **Aspect Ratio Shift** | Cut to 2.39:1 letterbox for 30-60 seconds (philosophical moment) then back to 16:9 | "The Deeper Layer" section only. Not more than once per video. |
| **Sound Design Shift** | Add ambient SFX (rain, distant sirens, industrial hum) that wasn't present before | Scene transitions, entering a new section of the argument |

### Open Loop Strategies

An open loop is a promise of future information that keeps the viewer watching to get the payoff.

**Verbal Open Loops (Scatter Throughout Script):**

| Position | Open Loop Example | Closes At |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| 0:30 | "And by the end of this video, I'll show you the one tool that changes everything." | 9:00 (The Move) |
| 2:00 | "Number three on this list is the one that kept me up last night." | 4:30 (when item #3 arrives) |
| 4:00 | "But the real story isn't any of these. It's what connects them. We'll get there." | 8:30 (The Deeper Layer) |
| 5:00 | "I'm going to say something in a minute that might change how you see your phone." | 6:30 (next list item or stat) |

**Rules:**
- Maximum 3 verbal open loops per video. More than that feels manipulative.
- ALWAYS close every loop you open. Unclosed loops = broken trust = unsubscribe.
- The open loop must be TRUE. Don't promise "the one thing" and then deliver something generic.

### Re-engagement Hooks (For Viewers Drifting)

These are micro-moments designed to snap wandering attention back.

| Hook Type | Example | Effect |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Unexpected Stat** | "34 times. That's how much more likely." (giant number on screen) | Numbers interrupt scanning behavior |
| **Personal Anchor** | "I watched this happen to someone I know." | Shifts from abstract to personal — viewers re-engage |
| **Challenge** | "If you think this doesn't apply to you, check your credit score right now." | Makes it interactive — viewer acts |
| **Contrast** | "They spent $3 billion building this. The workers who labeled the data got $2 an hour." | Juxtaposition creates tension |
| **Prediction** | "In 18 months, this won't be a conversation. It'll be a fact." | Future stakes re-engage present attention |

### Retention Monitoring (Post-Publish SOP)

**24 Hours After Publish:**
1. Open YouTube Studio > Analytics > Audience Retention for the video
2. Look for the drop-off cliffs — where does the line plummet?
3. Note the exact timestamp of each major drop (>5% sudden loss)

**Common Drop Patterns and Fixes:**

| Drop Pattern | What It Means | Fix for Next Video |
|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| 30%+ drop at 0:05 | Thumbnail/title promised something the video didn't deliver immediately | Match the first 5 seconds to the thumbnail/title promise more explicitly |
| Steady decline 0:30-2:00 | Context section is too slow or too vague | Get to the list faster. Cut context to 60 seconds max. |
| Drop at item #2 or #3 | List items aren't differentiated enough — viewer feels they've "gotten the idea" | Make each item visually distinct. Harder cuts between items. Escalate importance. |
| Drop at 7:00-8:00 | "The Deeper Layer" doesn't feel earned or isn't interesting enough | Make the deeper layer genuinely surprising — not a recap of what was already said |
| Spike at end screen | Viewers clicking the end screen recommendation | This is GOOD. It means they're staying engaged. Optimize end screen cards. |
| No spike at end screen | Viewers leaving before end screen | Your close is too long. Tighten it. "Pay attention." Fade to black. Done. |

**Weekly Retention Review:**
- Compare retention curves across all videos published that week
- Identify which retention anchors consistently work (and which don't)
- Build a "what works" library in Airtable — tag successful hooks, stats, interrupts

## 6. END SCREEN AND CARDS

### End Screen Template (Last 20 Seconds)

```
Visual: Red Nose sitting still, bowtie visible, slow fade to Dark Steel black
Audio: Tyrone's final line + 2 seconds silence + ambient fade

End Screen Elements (20-second window):
- LEFT: "Best for viewer" video suggestion (auto-selected by YouTube)
- RIGHT: Subscribe button (Red Nose silhouette + channel name)
- Both elements appear at the 10-second mark of the end screen window
```

**Rules:**
- Plan the last 20 seconds of EVERY video to accommodate end screen elements
- No important VO or visuals in the final 20 seconds — just the close + fade
- The "best for viewer" suggestion outperforms "specific video" selection in most cases
- Use "specific video" only when there's a clear sequel or related deep dive

### Info Cards (Mid-Video Links)

Place 2-3 info cards per long-form video:

| Position | Card Type | When To Use |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| 2:00-3:00 | Link to related video | When referencing a topic covered in a previous episode |
| 5:00-6:00 | Link to playlist | When the current topic spans multiple episodes |
| 8:00-9:00 | Link to related video | When "The Deeper Layer" connects to another episode |

**Rules:**
- Never place a card in the first 60 seconds (let the viewer commit to THIS video first)
- Never place more than 3 cards (it feels spammy)
- Card text should be natural: "I went deeper on this in Episode X" — not "CLICK HERE"
