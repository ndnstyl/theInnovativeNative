## 2. CROSS-PLATFORM DECISIONS

### Platform-by-Platform Verdict

**YOUTUBE (Primary Platform) — ALREADY DECIDED**

---

**TIKTOK — YES (Cross-Post Shorts, Near-Zero Marginal Cost)**

Verdict: Cross-post YouTube Shorts to TikTok with minimal modification.

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Marginal cost | Near-zero. Shorts are already produced. Upload takes 2 minutes. |
| Audience overlap | Low. TikTok skews younger (18-24) vs. YouTube (25-44). Different discovery. |
| Content fit | Excellent. Faceless, caption-driven, short-form. Made for TikTok. |
| Risk | Low. No extra production. If it works, free growth. If it doesn't, 2 minutes wasted. |

**TikTok Execution:**

- Account: `@BowTieBullies`
- Bio: "AI through the lens of someone who's survived broken systems. Full episodes on YouTube."
- Profile pic: Same Red Nose P2
- Upload schedule: Same day as YouTube Shorts, 2-4 hours later
- Modifications from YouTube Shorts:
  - Remove any "subscribe" CTAs (replace with "follow" if text overlay is editable)
  - Add TikTok-native sounds only if it doesn't compromise the brand (99% of the time, keep original audio)
  - Captions: Already baked into the video. No changes needed.
- Hashtags: #AI #ArtificialIntelligence #FYP #Tech #Future #DigitalSurvival

**TikTok-Specific Optimization:**
- First frame: The hook text must be readable in browse (same as Shorts)
- Duration sweet spot: 30-45 seconds (TikTok's algorithm currently favors this range over 15s or 60s)
- If a TikTok breaks 100K views, create 2-3 follow-up TikToks on the same topic (TikTok rewards topic consistency more than YouTube does)
- Reply to comments with video responses (TikTok-native feature) when a comment asks a good question

**Time cost: 10-15 minutes per week. Three uploads, paste captions, done.**

---

**TWITTER/X — YES (Tyrone Hot Takes + Clip Sharing, Light Lift)**

Verdict: Use Twitter/X as Tyrone's commentary layer. React to AI news in real-time. Share clips. Build an audience that comes to YouTube for the full breakdown.

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Marginal cost | Low. 5-10 minutes per day. No production needed. |
| Audience overlap | Medium. Tech/AI Twitter is large and active. Overlaps with YouTube AI audience. |
| Content fit | Excellent. Tyrone's voice is BUILT for short-form text. One-liners hit. |
| Risk | Low. Light lift. Easy to scale back if ROI is low. |

**Twitter/X Execution:**

- Account: `@BowTieBullies`
- Bio: "AI through the lens of someone who's survived broken systems. New episodes Tuesdays on YouTube."
- Pinned tweet: EP01 link (update to latest flagship episode monthly)
- Profile pic: Red Nose P5 (The Close) — works best at small circular crop
- Banner: Same as YouTube (Red Nose P4 silhouette, adjusted to 1500x500px)

**Daily Output (Total: 10-15 min/day):**

| Activity | Volume | Time |
|----------|--------|------|
| Original hot take tweet | 1 per day | 2 min |
| Quote tweet of AI news | 1-2 per day | 3-5 min |
| Reply to relevant threads | 3-5 per day | 5-8 min |
| Video clip share | 1 per week (on publish day) | 2 min |

**Content Types for Twitter:**

1. **One-liner hot takes** (Tyrone's bread and butter):
   ```
   The algorithm doesn't know your name.
   But it knows your zip code.
   And that's enough.
   ```

2. **Quote tweets of AI news** (react, don't just share):
   ```
   [Quote tweet: "OpenAI announces new model..."]
   "Ask who tested it. Ask what neighborhoods the training data came from.
   Then ask who profits. Same answer every time."
   ```

3. **Thread (rare, 1 per week max)** — a 3-5 tweet breakdown of one topic:
   ```
   1/ They call it "disruption."
   Where I'm from, disruption isn't a business model. It's what happens when the lights go out.

   2/ AI is disrupting 300 million jobs globally. That's Goldman's number, not mine.
   But Goldman isn't losing their jobs. They're building the models.

   3/ The hood already knows what disruption looks like.
   We've been disrupted by every system that was "just trying to help."

   New episode is up. Link in bio.
   ```

4. **Clip sharing** (Tuesday, after publish):
   - Upload a 30-45 second clip from the new episode directly to Twitter (native video, NOT a YouTube link — native video gets 10x the reach)
   - Caption: 1-2 lines + "Full episode on YouTube."

**Twitter Rules:**
- Never beg for follows or retweets
- Never use more than 2 hashtags per tweet (ideally zero)
- Never thread-dump 10+ tweets. 3-5 max.
- Ratio: 80% commentary and engagement / 20% own content
- When posting own content: state it plainly, no hype
- Engage with AI/tech accounts that have 10K-100K followers (the mid-tier accounts are more likely to engage back than the million-follower ones)

---

**LINKEDIN — NO (Defer to Month 6+)**

Verdict: Wrong audience for launch. Revisit when the brand has proof of traction.

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Audience | Corporate professionals, career-focused. Not BowTie Bullies' Day 1 audience. |
| Content fit | Tyrone's voice does NOT work in LinkedIn's "thought leader" format. It would require voice dilution. |
| Marginal cost | High. LinkedIn requires a different content format (text posts, articles, carousels). Not repurposable from existing content. |
| ROI at launch | Near-zero. LinkedIn audiences need social proof before they engage with a new creator. No videos, no subs = no credibility on LinkedIn. |

**Month 6+ Reassessment:** If BowTie Bullies hits 25K+ YouTube subscribers AND there's corporate interest (speaking, consulting, partnerships), create a LinkedIn presence with a separate content strategy. Tyrone's voice can adapt to LinkedIn IF the audience earns it first.

---

**THREADS — NO**

Verdict: Duplicate of Twitter effort with less AI/tech audience concentration. Not worth the split attention.

**FACEBOOK — NO**

### Playlist Naming Conventions

| Rule | Example | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| Season playlists include season name | "Season 1: The Marathon" | Brand consistency with episode guide |
| Topic playlists use plain language | "AI & The System" not "Pillar 1 Content" | Viewers don't know your internal naming |
| Subtitles add context | "Real Impact, No Theory" | Differentiates from generic AI playlists |
| No emojis in playlist titles | "The Gear" not "The Gear" | Brand consistency — BowTie Bullies doesn't use emojis |

### Playlist Description Template

```
[1 sentence: What this playlist covers]
[1 sentence: Why it matters / what makes this perspective different]
[1 sentence: What the viewer will get from watching]

BowTie Bullies — The Aftermath
```

### Playlist Ordering Rules

| Playlist Type | Ordering | Rationale |
|--------------|----------|-----------|
| Season playlists | Manual: oldest first (EP01 at top) | Sequential viewing. Start from the beginning. |
| Topic playlists | Newest first | Returning viewers want the latest. New viewers will browse. |
| "Start Here" | Manual: oldest first | Onboarding. Watch in order. |
| "News Reactions" | Newest first | Time-sensitive. Latest reaction is most relevant. |
| "The Gear" | Manual: grouped by category | Viewers looking for a specific product type can scan. |

### Playlist Maintenance SOP

| Frequency | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Every Tuesday | Add new long-form episode to: Start Here + Season playlist + Topic playlist |
| Every Wednesday | Add news reaction to: News Reactions playlist |
| Every Saturday | Add product feature to: The Gear playlist |
| Monthly | Review playlist ordering — move best-performing videos higher in topic playlists |
| Per season | Create new season playlist when first episode of new season publishes |
| Quarterly | Audit all playlists — remove any unlisted/deleted videos, check descriptions are current |

### Playlist Promotion

- **End screens:** When a video belongs to a playlist, use "Playlist" as one of the two end screen elements (instead of "Best for viewer")
- **Pinned comments:** When a video is the start of a multi-part topic, pin a comment: "This is part of the [Playlist Name] series. Watch them all: [playlist link]"
- **Description:** Add a "Playlists" section to the channel description/about page listing all 8 playlists
- **Community Tab:** Monthly post: "If you're new here, start with [Start Here playlist link]. Then go deep with [AI & The System playlist link]."

## 5. PLATFORM PRIORITY MATRIX

When time is limited (and it always is), prioritize in this order:

```
PRIORITY 1 (Never Skip):
  YouTube long-form publish (Tuesday)
  YouTube Shorts (3/week)
  YouTube comment responses (first 48 hours)

PRIORITY 2 (High Value, Low Effort):
  IG Reels (cross-posted shorts)
  TikTok (cross-posted shorts)
  IG Stories (daily, 5-10 min each)

PRIORITY 3 (Important but Deferrable):
  Twitter/X hot takes and engagement
  Community Tab posts
  IG Carousels
  Reddit discussion posts

PRIORITY 4 (Do When Capacity Allows):
  Reddit deep engagement (commenting on others' posts)
  Twitter thread content
  IG Feed single-image posts
  Email newsletter (Month 3+)
```

If you only have 30 minutes in a day for distribution: YouTube comments + IG Story + 1 tweet. That's it. The rest can wait.
