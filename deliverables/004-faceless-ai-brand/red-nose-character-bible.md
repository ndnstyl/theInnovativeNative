# Red Nose — Character Bible

> The face of a faceless channel. An American Red Nose Pitbull — gentleman strategist, controlled intelligence — who chose composure over chaos.

---

## Identity

**Full Name:** Red Nose
**Breed:** American Red Nose Pitbull (APBT)
**Role:** Visual anchor, brand mascot, narrative witness
**Relationship to Tyrone:** Silent companion. He mirrors Tyrone's energy — composed, alert, unbothered by noise, watching everything.

### Tyrone — Name Protocol

Tyrone's crew never calls him by his actual name. He is addressed as:
- **"T"** — the default
- **"Unk"** — respect, elder status
- **"OG"** — earned authority

If someone slips and calls him "Tyrone" — they get the stare. The fiercest, deadliest "if looks could kill" stare-down. No words. Just eyes. The kind of silence that makes the room recalibrate. It's happened. It doesn't happen twice.

Red Nose is not a cartoon mascot. He's rendered in 2D cel-shaded style — cinematic, dramatic, with clean lines and intentional stylization. He sits in the same world as the content — dark steel, rust light, urban textures. He's not cute. He's composed. His energy is controlled intelligence — a gentleman strategist, not a beast.

## The BowTie Mark (Brand Symbol)

The BowTie Mark is the Chevrolet bowtie silhouette — the unifying brand symbol. It does NOT appear on Red Nose's body.

```
Shape:       Chevrolet bowtie silhouette (horizontal diamond-cross)
Fill:        Matte black (#1E1E20 Dark Steel)
Trim:        Ultra-thin deep crimson red (#8B0000) — brake-light red, NOT bright/neon
Thickness:   Trim border is 3-5% of bowtie height
```

### Where the BowTie Mark Appears
| Location | Context |
|----------|---------|
| Vehicle grille emblems | Blacked-out vehicles with crimson-trimmed bowtie |
| Environmental easter eggs | Subtle placement in backgrounds, on walls, stickers |
| Channel watermark | 150x150px, transparent, persistent corner element |
| Merch/branding | Channel icon, thumbnails, end screens |

### Where the BowTie Mark NEVER Appears
- NOT on Red Nose's body (no thigh patch, no belt buckle, no glove, no chest)
- NOT as clothing or accessory on the character
- NOT as a literal fabric bowtie anywhere

### BowTie Mark Rules
1. **NEVER a literal bowtie at the collar.** No fabric, no canvas, no tie.
2. **NEVER on Red Nose's body.** The mark is environmental and branding only.
3. **Always the Chevrolet silhouette shape** — clean, geometric, matte.
4. **Deep crimson trim only** — never bright red, never neon, never glowing.
5. **≤5% of visual frame** — the mark is a detail, not a feature.
6. **Matte finish always** — no shine, no gloss, no metallic reflection.

**What the BowTie Mark means:** "I chose to be better than how you see me." Strength that chose restraint. Dignity that wasn't given — it was decided. The mark is earned, not displayed.

## Generation Tips

### Lighting
- Always single source light (sodium vapor orange preferred)
- Roger Deakins style — dramatic, intentional shadows
- Never flat/even lighting. Never studio white.

### Environment
- Urban: concrete, steel, chain-link, brick, rust
- Never: grass, suburbs, parks, clean interiors
- Weather OK: rain, fog, cold breath visible — adds drama

### Post-Processing
- Film grain: 15-25% opacity
- Vignette: heavy, 30-50% edge darkening
- Desaturation: -20 to -30%
- Contrast: +15 to +20%
- No HDR. No glow. No lens flare.

### Common Generation Failures (Watch For)
| Issue | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Black nose instead of red/liver | Add "distinctive red nose, liver-colored nose" explicitly |
| Cropped ears | Add "natural uncropped rose ears" and negative "cropped ears" |
| Too playful/cute expression | Strengthen "composed, dignified, NOT playful" in prompt |
| Missing coveralls/boots | Add "sleeveless black coveralls slightly unzipped, black tactical boots" explicitly |
| Suburban background | Add negative "suburban, grass, yard, park, clean" |
| Multiple dogs | Add negative "multiple dogs, pack, other animals" |
| Missing scar | Add "small healed scar across left brow" early in prompt |
