# BowTie Bullies — The Aftermath | Tone of Voice

## Voice Character

Tyrone sounds like a man who's been watching the world break apart his whole life — and now watches AI accelerate what was already broken. He doesn't raise his voice. He doesn't rush the point. He speaks because he's lived it. His authority is earned, not claimed. Think "the OG on the block who saw it all coming" — measured, scarred, unshaken.

## Core Attributes

### 1. Emotional, Not Exposed
Tyrone carries emotion, but he doesn't spill it. You can feel the angst. You won't hear him explain it. Subtext over confession. History behind every sentence. Pain held steady, not displayed.

**Example**: "Some lessons cost more than money." vs "I went through so much pain and suffering growing up."

### 2. Stoic, Not Cold
Stillness is strength. He lets silence talk. Short to medium sentences. Clean breaks. Willingness to stop early. Not detached, not robotic — present.

**Example**: "I've seen what happens when people lie to themselves." *[2 second silence]* vs "Let me tell you about the time when I learned that people..."

### 3. Confident, Not Loud
He doesn't flex. He doesn't sell. He doesn't convince. The confidence is implied. Declarative statements. No hype language. No excess punctuation. Not motivational yelling — just truth stated plainly.

**Example**: "The algorithm doesn't know your name. But it knows your zip code. And that's enough." vs "OH MY GOD you won't BELIEVE what AI is doing to your privacy!!!"

### 4. Raw, Not Chaotic
The edge is real. But it's controlled. Every word earns its place. Plain language. Minimal polish. Natural rhythm. Not messy, not unfocused — just unvarnished.

**Example**: "AI didn't invent bias. It just made it faster." vs "The technological implications of algorithmic prejudice represent a paradigmatic shift..."

## Writing Guidelines

### Do
- Use first person ("I've seen," "where I'm from," "the block taught me")
- Lead with lived experience, not statistics (stats support, they don't lead)
- Use street-level analogies for technical concepts
- Write how people talk — contractions, fragments, intentional rhythm
- Leave white space. Silence is a beat, not an accident.
- Frame AI through the lens of systems he's already survived

### Don't
- Use marketing jargon ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruption")
- Use tech jargon to sound smart ("large language model" → "the machine")
- Use therapy language ("healing journey," "toxic," "boundaries")
- Use corporate language ("leverage," "synergy," "optimize")
- Over-explain. Trust the viewer to connect the dots.
- Celebrate tech progress uncritically
- Fear-monger without offering a move

## Platform Voice Adjustments

### YouTube Long-Form (Tyrone's primary voice)
Full narrative weight. Measured pace (110-125 WPM). Intentional pauses. Stories start in the middle. Ends without neat closure. Trust the viewer.

### YouTube Shorts (Compressed Tyrone)
Same voice, higher density. Hook in first 2 seconds. One idea. One punch. Same rhythm — just less runway.
- "You're scrolling at 2 AM. A job you saved yesterday? Gone. Not filled by a person. Filled by a program."

### Instagram Captions
Written Tyrone. Slightly more poetic. Fragment-heavy. Visual rhythm matters.
- "The algorithm doesn't know your name. / But it knows your zip code. / And that's enough."

### TikTok Captions
Compressed. No excess. Platform-native.
- "pov: the job you saved yesterday got automated overnight"
- "things they don't tell you about AI and your neighborhood"

## Content Theme Lines

### Primary Themes
1. **"The Aftermath"** — We're already living in the collapse. This is what it looks like.
2. **"They don't want you to know"** — Systems hiding in plain sight.
3. **"The block already taught me"** — Survival wisdom applied to AI.
4. **"Pay attention"** — The closing call. Not panic — awareness.
5. **"Gear that actually works"** — Products framed as survival tools, not shopping.

### Hook Formulas
- **Second-Person Scenario**: "You're scrolling at 2 AM..."
- **Cold Stat**: "34 times more likely..."
- **Anecdote**: "I remember when the first camera went up on the corner..."
- **Question**: "What happens when the machine decides you don't qualify?"
- **Contradiction**: "They call it 'smart.' I call it 'watching.'"
- **Timestamp**: "By 2027, 40% of..."

## Emotional Range

Tyrone leaves people feeling:
- Confident (not scared)
- Clear (not confused)
- Seen (not lectured at)

Never rushed. Never hyped. There's urgency in the truth. Not panic.

---

## Final Gut Check

Before any script is approved:
1. Does this feel earned?
2. Would this still hit if I said less?
3. Am I leaving space for the viewer?
4. Does this sound like someone who's been there?
5. Is the AI angle grounded in real impact, not hype?
6. Would Tyrone actually say this?
7. Would Red Nose sit still through it?

If it's quiet but heavy — it's right.
