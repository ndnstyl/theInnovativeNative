## Understanding Traditional Marketing Analytics

Traditional marketing analytics methods often involve manual data analysis. This process is time-consuming and struggles to handle the rapid flow of incoming data [6].

This approach creates significant challenges, including siloed teams and underutilization of data. Creative and analytical teams often operate separately, failing to leverage data as a valuable asset [2, 4]. The complexities of manual analysis make it difficult to accurately determine **marketing attribution**.

Predicting future performance accurately is also a struggle. Agencies that rely on intuition often face errors and difficulties in forecasting behaviors within competitive environments [5, 6].
