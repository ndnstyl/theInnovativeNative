## Post 3: Technical Credibility (RAG vs GPT)

**Character count**: 1,312

---

Everyone's talking about GPT.

While building AI tools for law firms, I kept hearing the same concern:

"It invents citations. We can't use that."

Here's the technical difference that matters:

**Generic LLM (GPT-style):**
- Generates text from training data
- "Knows" law the way it knows everything — vaguely
- Invents citations when uncertain
- Treats all sources equally

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):**
- Retrieves from actual documents first
- Generates answers from verified sources only
- Can't cite what it didn't find
- Can be configured to weight sources differently

A litigation partner explained what they needed:

"We need the system to know that Supreme Court precedent matters more than a district court opinion. And we need to see where every answer came from."

That's what we built. Their briefs and memos first. Verified case law second. Full audit trail.

We're focused on 3 practice areas to start: Bankruptcy, Criminal Procedure, Administrative Law.

Not because we're experts in those areas — because that's where our early partners practice.
