## The 5 Red Flags in Every Demo

When you sit through a sales pitch, listen for these. If the vendor dodges any of them, walk.

1. **"Can you show me a live generation on a case I brought?"** — If they want to use their canned demo, they are hiding output quality. A real tool runs on real records in real time.
2. **"Who owns the data after it enters your system?"** — If the answer is "we retain it for training," your privileged info just left your firm. Hard no after Heppner.
3. **"What is your hallucination rate on citations?"** — Published benchmarks or walk. "Very low" is not a number.
4. **"Show me the paralegal review workflow."** — If the tool replaces human review entirely, it is pre-Heppner thinking. The right answer is "AI drafts, paralegal verifies, attorney signs."
5. **"What integrations do you have with my CMS?"** — Filevine, Clio, CasePeer, SmartAdvocate, CloudLex. If your CMS is not on their list, the friction will sink the rollout.

## Decision Tree: Which Tool Fits Your Firm

### Small firm (under 10 attorneys, under 20 demand letters/mo)

**Start with Tavrn.** $299/mo entry, fast onboarding, Clio + CasePeer integrations. You will see ROI inside 30 days without a six-month vendor negotiation.

### Mid-size firm (10-25 attorneys, 20-50 letters/mo)

**Tavrn or Filevine DemandsAI.** If you are already on Filevine, use DemandsAI — no new vendor, no new contract, no new security review. If you are on Clio or CasePeer, Tavrn is the path of least resistance.

### Large firm (25+ attorneys, 50+ letters/mo)

**Supio or pilot EvenUp.** Supio gives you configurability without enterprise pricing. EvenUp is the gold standard if you have the volume to justify the cost — but start with a paid pilot on a single practice area before the full rollout.

### Mass tort shop

**EvenUp or build custom.** At mass tort volume, the math on EvenUp makes sense. Below EvenUp's price point, most mass tort firms benefit from a custom RAG pipeline on their own CMS — that is a 3-6 month project, not a vendor purchase.
