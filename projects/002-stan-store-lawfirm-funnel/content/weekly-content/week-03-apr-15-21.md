---

### LinkedIn Post 1 (Text - Insight)

**Post Body:**

When most PI attorneys hear "AI in law," they picture ChatGPT writing a motion to dismiss. Then they picture the 729 attorneys who got caught filing hallucinated cases. Then they close the laptop.

That is the wrong picture.

The AI actually transforming PI practice has nothing to do with drafting briefs. It handles the operational bottlenecks that eat 48% of an attorney's workday. Four specific ones.

Here is the play. Four things to audit this week.

One. Pull your last 10 closed case files. Time how long your paralegal spent on medical record review. The industry average is 8 to 12 hours per file. If yours is close to that, book a 30-minute demo with Supio or Medex AI. Bring a real de-identified record. Ask them to summarize it live. If they hedge, walk. Tools in this category deliver 90% time savings and surface 3x more relevant evidence than manual review.

Two. Check what your firm uses for case valuation. If the answer is "attorney judgment," you are fighting Colossus with a gut feeling. Colossus is the AI insurance companies use to undervalue claims. Tools like EvenUp and CasePeer have settlement data across tens of thousands of cases. Run your last 5 closed cases through a free trial and compare the AI valuation to your final settlement. The gap is your data point.

Three. Pull your intake data from the last 90 days. What percentage of $442 leads got routed to a closer within 5 minutes? If you do not have that number, your intake is a bottleneck. AI intake scoring tools like Filevine or Lawmatics flag high-value leads in real time and route them before they go cold. Set a benchmark this week.

Four. For mass tort firms: check your plaintiff fact sheet completion rate. If it is below 70%, you have a client communication problem. AI communication platforms see 85% PFS completion rates. One platform conversation costs less than one paralegal follow-up call.

82% of AI-using lawyers report increased efficiency. The math is not complicated. The bottleneck is identifying where to start.

### LinkedIn Post 1 (Text - Insight)

**Post Body:**

If someone tries to sell you "AI for your law firm" and the product is just ChatGPT with a different skin, walk away.

Here is the distinction every PI firm owner needs to understand before spending a dollar on legal AI.

A ChatGPT wrapper takes your prompt, sends it to a general-purpose AI model, and returns whatever that model generates. The model was trained on the open internet. It does not know your case law. It does not know your jurisdiction. It hallucinates citations. And after Heppner, anything you input may waive privilege.

A RAG system is fundamentally different. RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation. Instead of generating answers from its training data, it retrieves information from a database you control, then uses AI to summarize and present that information.

Here is why that matters for PI.

A RAG system connected to a settlement database does not hallucinate case values. It retrieves actual settlements. Cervical strain, Maricopa County, 2024. $47,000 average. Real data. Not a guess.

A RAG system connected to your medical records does not invent diagnoses. It finds the ICD-10 codes, treatment notes, and prognosis statements that are actually in the records.

A RAG system connected to statute databases does not hallucinate citations. It retrieves the actual statute text.

The data stays in your environment. The AI never sees it during training. Privilege is preserved. Accuracy is verifiable.

This is the distinction that separates the 729 hallucinated filing disasters from the 82% of AI-using lawyers who report increased efficiency.

When you evaluate legal AI tools, ask one question: does this tool retrieve from verified data, or does it generate from a general-purpose model? That answer determines whether you are adopting a competitive advantage or a malpractice risk.
