# 07 — Character & Wardrobe Design

## Production: "The Man Who Never Raised His Voice"
## Runtime: 5:00 | Format: 16:9 YouTube | Style: Hybrid Cinematic-Brand

---

## Character Design Philosophy

Elias is a man who designed himself to disappear. His physical presence, clothing, posture, and movement are all **engineered normalcy**. But the engineering is the tell. A truly normal man doesn't work this hard at being unremarkable.

The character design must communicate this tension:
- **On the surface:** Working-class, mid-40s, nothing memorable
- **Underneath:** A physical frame that was built for something else, hands that remember what they've done, eyes that see more than they reveal

## Elias — Primary Character

### Physical Description

| Attribute | Description | AI Consistency Anchor |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Age | 42-48 | Middle-aged, some gray |
| Build | Medium-large frame, kept lean | Not muscular display — functional strength |
| Height | 5'11"–6'1" | Slightly taller than average — has to work to not stand out |
| Skin | Weathered, sun-touched | Hands especially — a man who works outside |
| Hair | Dark brown/black, some gray at temples | Short, practical, self-cut look |
| Facial hair | Light stubble or clean-shaven | Not styled — maintained |
| Eyes | Dark brown or gray-green | The most important feature — see below |
| Hands | Large, scarred knuckles (subtle) | The story is in his hands |
| Posture | Deliberately lowered shoulders | He learned to compress himself |
| Movement | Economical, no wasted motion | A man who is aware of every gesture |

### The Eyes — Critical Detail

Elias's eyes are the camera's primary subject. They must communicate three states:

1. **Default (Acts I, III — present):** Soft, slightly downcast, unthreatening. A dog that knows not to make eye contact. Warm but guarded.

2. **Past (Act II flashback):** Sharp, calculating, still. The same eyes, but the softness is removed. Looking AT things, not past them. Assessing.

3. **Activated (Act IV):** Neither soft nor sharp. Clear. Like a lens coming into focus. Not angry, not aggressive — simply present. Fully online.

### Physical Transformation Arc

| Act | Posture | Eye State | Movement Quality | Physical Read |
|-----|---------|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| I | Shoulders low, head slightly bowed | Soft, averted | Quiet, minimized, deferential | "Good guy. Soft." |
| II | Same, but awareness visible | Transitioning — we see flickers of the old gaze | Same measured pace on the trail | "Something is off" |
| III (Flash) | Shoulders square, chin level | Sharp, direct, unblinking | Precise, economical, threatening | "This man is dangerous" |
| III (Present) | Returns to low, practiced | Back to soft — but we know now | Deliberate reconstruction visible | "He's acting" |
| IV | Shoulders rise 2 inches. Chin lifts. | Full activation — clear, focused, absolute | Deliberate, controlled, unstoppable | "He was always this" |
| V | Settled — not low, not high. Natural. | At peace. The first honest eye contact. | Slower than ever, no performance | "He finally matches" |

### Face — AI Generation Consistency Token

For consistent character generation across multiple AI image prompts, use this **anchoring description block** in every Elias prompt:

```
A man in his mid-40s, dark brown hair with gray at the temples,
short practical haircut, light stubble, dark eyes,
weathered but handsome face with visible laugh lines,
medium-large build kept lean, broad shoulders,
scarred knuckles (subtle), working-class hands,
the face of a man who could be dangerous but has chosen not to be,
photorealistic, hyperdetailed skin texture, subsurface scattering
```

**Consistency Technique for AI Tools:**
- Use the SAME base image of Elias for all shots
- In Midjourney: use `--cref [reference_image_url]` for character reference
- In Flux/DALL-E: describe the character identically each time
- In Kling/Runway image-to-video: start from consistent base images
- Consider generating a **character sheet** first: front, 3/4, profile, back — then reference these for all subsequent generations

### Character Sheet Prompt (Generate First)
```
Character reference sheet of a man in his mid-40s,
dark brown hair with gray at temples, short practical haircut,
light stubble, dark brown eyes, weathered face,
wearing a dark navy work jacket over gray henley,

character design reference sheet layout
```

## Elias — Wardrobe

### Wardrobe Philosophy

Elias dresses to be forgotten. His clothes are:
- **Muted** — no patterns, no logos, no bright colors
- **Functional** — every item serves a purpose
- **Worn** — not new, not threadbare. Lived-in.
- **Consistent** — he likely wears the same few items on rotation

### Primary Outfit (Acts I, III, IV, V — Present Day)

| Item | Description | Color | Texture/Material |
|------|-------------|-------|------------------|
| Work jacket | Canvas or waxed cotton, hip-length | Dark navy or charcoal | Slightly faded, broken-in |
| Henley shirt | Long-sleeve, no buttons or 3 buttons | Medium gray or slate | Soft cotton, worn at collar and cuffs |
| Pants | Work pants or dark jeans | Dark indigo or black | Not fashion denim — work denim |
| Boots | Work boots, leather, lace-up | Dark brown | Resoled at least once, worn at the heel |
| Belt | Leather, simple buckle | Dark brown | Cracked slightly, functional |
| Watch | Simple analog, leather strap (or no watch) | Silver face, brown strap | Not expensive — a tool, not jewelry |

**What he does NOT wear:**
- No logos or brand marks
- No bright colors
- No jewelry (no ring — no evidence of partnership)
- No hat (his face is the story)
- No sunglasses
- Nothing attention-grabbing

### Secondary Outfit — Hardware Store (Act I, under jacket)

When inside the store, the jacket is off. We see:
| Item | Description | Color |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Henley (same) | Sleeves pushed to elbows | Gray/slate |
| Work apron | Optional — canvas tool apron | Tan/khaki |
| Boots (same) | — | Dark brown |

### Flashback Outfit (Act II–III — The Past)

| Item | Description | Color | Difference from Present |
|------|-------------|-------|------------------------|
| T-shirt | Fitted, crew neck | Black | Closer-fitting — body was a tool |
| Jacket | Leather or dark cotton, structured | Black or very dark brown | More deliberate — not "disappearing" |
| Pants | Dark tactical or fitted jeans | Black | More purposeful |
| Boots | Same style but newer | Darker | Less worn — this is before the years of walking |

**Key Difference:** In the past, Elias dressed with **intention toward capability**. Dark, fitted, nothing loose to grab. In the present, he dresses with **intention toward invisibility**. Looser, lighter in color (relatively), softer fabrics.

### The Night Walk (Acts I, II — Trail)

Same outfit as the workday — he walks in what he wore. The jacket is his only concession to the cold. He doesn't change for the walk because the walk is not an event. It's a practice.

## Supporting Characters

### The Woman at the Bridge

**Screen Time:** ~30 seconds (Shots 046, 055–056, 069–071)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Age | 30-40 |
| Build | Average, not physically imposing |
| Appearance | Normal — not stylized, not sexualized |
| Expression | Fear → relief → reverence |
| Clothing | Practical evening wear — a jacket over a blouse, jeans, walking shoes |
| Color palette | Muted (she's part of Elias's world, not a contrast) |
| Hair | Down, slightly disheveled from the confrontation |

**She is NOT:**
- A damsel in distress archetype — she's a person in a bad situation
- Styled or made-up — she was walking, not going out
- A plot device — she has agency (she tells Elias to go, she reports to police)

**AI Prompt Anchoring:**
```
A woman in her mid-30s, practical clothing — dark jacket,
light blouse, jeans, walking shoes, slightly disheveled,
natural appearance, no makeup visible,
expression of controlled fear transitioning to relief,
photorealistic, not stylized, not glamorous
```

### The Two Drunk Men

**Screen Time:** ~25 seconds (Shots 045, 059, 064, 066)

| Attribute | Man 1 | Man 2 |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Age | 28-35 | 25-32 |
| Build | Stocky, aggressive | Lean, wiry |
| Posture | Leaning, territorial | Pacing, agitated |
| Clothing | Bar clothes — untucked shirt, jeans | Similar — hoodie, jeans |
| Expression | Dominant → amused → confused → recognition | Following Man 1's lead throughout |
| Color palette | Slightly warmer than Elias's world — reds, browns | Same |

**They are:**
- Recognizably dangerous in an ordinary way — bar aggression, not criminal masterminds
- Initially confident (body language open, laughing, territorial)
- Then recognition-shifted (body language closing, stepping back, faces draining)

**AI Prompt Anchoring:**
```
Two men in their early 30s, bar clothes — untucked shirts, jeans,
one stocky leaning aggressively, one lean and wiry,
drunk body language, confrontational postures,
lit from above by harsh cold bridge light,
their faces shift from amusement to something deeper,
photorealistic, uncomfortable realism
```

### The Police Officer

**Screen Time:** ~5 seconds (Shot 072)

| Attribute | Description |

### Townspeople (Background/Brief)

**Screen Time:** 10-15 seconds combined (Shots 009–011, 015)

| Type | Description | Purpose |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Gossiper 1 | Two people on sidewalk, gesturing toward store | Delivers "Good guy" |
| Gossiper 2 | Person with slight smirk | Delivers "Soft, though" |
| Gossiper 3 | Woman at cafe counter | Delivers "Would not hurt a fly" |

All townspeople should look **normal, small-town, unremarkable** — because that is what they think about Elias, and they're wrong.

## Wardrobe Color Continuity

### Color Rules Across All Characters

| Character | Color Range | Saturation | Purpose |
|-----------|-------------|------------|---------|
| Elias (present) | Navy, gray, slate, dark brown | Low | Disappearing |
| Elias (past) | Black, near-black | Low | Capable, intentional |
| The Woman | Muted earth tones | Medium-low | Part of Elias's world |
| The Drunk Men | Slightly warmer — reds, browns | Medium | Contrast with the cold world |
| Townspeople | Mixed but all muted | Medium | The palette of ordinary life |
| Police Officer | Dark navy uniform | Standard | Official, institutional |

**Nobody wears cyan or magenta.** The brand colors exist only in light — never in fabric, never in objects, never in the built world.

## Character Physicality Notes for AI Video Generation

### Elias — Movement Direction for Image-to-Video

When generating image-to-video clips of Elias, direct the motion with these principles:

**Walking:**
- Pace: 2.5 steps per second (normal), 1.5 steps per second (final walk)
- Arms: At sides, slight natural swing, never in pockets
- Head: Level, slightly forward-weighted, eyes ahead not down
- Shoulders: Low, compressed (present day) vs. natural (Act IV onward)

**Standing Still:**
- Weight: Even on both feet, never shifting
- Hands: At sides. Not crossed, not in pockets. Available.
- Breathing: Visible in cold air — slow, controlled
- Face: Minimum movement. He communicates with stillness.

**The Step Forward (Act IV):**
- Single step: weight transfer from back foot to front
- The step covers more ground than expected (long stride)
- Hands remain at sides — they don't ball into fists
- Chin raises 1 inch — the only upward movement

**Elias Speaking ("Step away from her"):**
- Mouth opens minimally — not a shout
- No hand gestures, no pointing
- Eye contact: direct, fixed, unwavering
- The words come from the chest, not the throat

### The Drunk Men — Movement Direction

**Before Recognition:**
- Loose, wide movements (drunk body language)
- Laughing involves full body — leaning back, slapping
- Territorial — arms wide, taking up space

**After Recognition (Shot 066):**
- Movement contracts rapidly
- Arms come to sides or cross protectively
- Weight shifts backward
- Eyes widen, then narrow
- The laugh dies mid-expression

### The Woman — Movement Direction

**During Confrontation:**
- Pressed against railing — small, compressed
- Hands gripping railing behind her
- Head shaking (telling Elias to go)
- Mouth moves but minimal — whispering

**After Confrontation:**
- Shoulders drop (tension releasing)
- Hands come off the railing
- Breathing visible (gasping, then steadying)
- Eyes on Elias — not leaving him

## AI Character Consistency Workflow

### Step 1: Generate the Character Sheet
Use the character sheet prompt (above) to create Elias's reference images. Generate 4-6 variations and select the one that best matches the description.

### Step 2: Lock the Reference
Save the chosen image as `elias-reference.png`. All subsequent image generations must reference this.

### Step 3: Per-Shot Generation
For each shot in the shot list, combine:
1. The shot description from [03-shot-list-camera.md](03-shot-list-camera.md)
2. The lighting spec from [04-lighting-design.md](04-lighting-design.md)
3. The environment from [06-production-design-sets.md](06-production-design-sets.md)
4. The character anchoring block (above)
5. The wardrobe for the relevant act

### Step 4: Consistency Check
After generating each batch of images, compare against the reference:
- Face structure consistent?
- Hair color/style consistent?
- Build/proportions consistent?
- Wardrobe appropriate for the act?
- If not, regenerate with more explicit anchoring

### Step 5: Image-to-Video
Feed consistent base images into Kling/Runway/MiniMax with motion direction from the physicality notes above. The base image consistency is the foundation — the video generation adds motion to an already-consistent character.
