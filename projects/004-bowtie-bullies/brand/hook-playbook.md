# BOWTIE BULLIES — Cold Open Hook Playbook

## Gap 1.1.3 | 6 Archetypes x 3 Examples = 18 Production-Ready Hooks

---

## How to Use This File

Every BowTie Bullies video — long-form, short, reel — opens cold. No intro. No brand mention. No "hey guys." The first words the viewer hears determine whether they stay or scroll. These hooks are the first 10-20 seconds of any piece of content.

**Selection Process:**
1. Identify your content type (News Reaction, Deep Dive, Product, Long-Form)
2. Match the emotional register to the topic
3. Pick the archetype that fits
4. Plug the example directly into your script template or adapt it

**Voice Rules (Non-Negotiable):**
- WPM: 110-120 (measured, not rushed)
- No hype. No uptalk. No vocal fry.
- Volume drops = emphasis (not rises)
- Pauses carry weight. If a line is heavy, let it stand alone.
- Tyrone sounds like someone who has been there. Not someone who read about it.

**SSML Model:** `eleven_multilingual_v2`
**Voice Settings:** Stability 0.62 / Similarity 0.78 / Style 0.15 / Speaker Boost ON

**Best for:** Long-Form cold opens, Deep Dive Shorts, News Reactions tied to daily life
**Episode Tags:** EP 01 Victory Lap, EP 04 Grinding All My Life, EP 06 Crenshaw, EP 14 Double Up, EP 15 No Pressure

## EMOTIONAL REGISTER GLOSSARY

| Register | What It Feels Like | Tyrone's Delivery |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Immersion** | You're inside the moment | Slow pace, soft volume, second person |
| **Weight** | The number is too large to process | Pause after the stat, let it land |
| **Unease** | Something is wrong but you can't name it | Lower volume, slower rate, trailing sentences |
| **Recognition** | "That happened to me" | Normal pace, direct tone, plain language |
| **Curiosity** | "Wait, what?" | Question delivery, pause for absorption |
| **Discomfort** | You're complicit and you know it | Soft volume, deliberate pacing, let silence accuse |
| **Controlled anger** | Not rage. Clarity with an edge. | Steady pace, emphasis on key words, no volume increase |
| **Bitter clarity** | The irony is obvious and it hurts | Slightly faster pace, then sudden stop |
| **Quiet grief** | Loss that doesn't need to be loud | Softest volume, longest pauses, fewest words |
| **Urgency** | Not panic. Awareness that time matters. | Slightly elevated pace, no pauses until the landing |

## RULES FOR WRITING NEW HOOKS

When creating hooks not in this playbook, follow these constraints:

1. **2-4 sentences maximum.** A hook is not a monologue. It's a door.
2. **10-20 seconds at 115 WPM.** If it's longer, it's not a hook. It's a cold open.
3. **No jargon.** Tyrone says "the machine" not "large language model." See voice guide translation table.
4. **No hype language.** No "mind-blowing," "insane," "game-changing," "you won't believe."
5. **No questions that answer themselves.** Bad: "Did you know AI can read your face?" Good: "The camera above the door doesn't just record anymore."
6. **Ground the abstract.** Every hook connects to a place, a person, a time, or a number. Never float in theory.
7. **End with weight.** The last line of the hook should be the heaviest. The viewer carries it into the content.
8. **Silence is part of the hook.** A 1-1.5 second pause after the hook landing is not dead air. It's the hook completing.
9. **Tyrone doesn't announce.** He doesn't say "today we're going to talk about." He's already talking about it. The viewer arrived mid-conversation.
10. **One emotional register per hook.** Don't mix curiosity and anger. Pick one. Commit.
