# Tyrone's Voice

## Voice in One Sentence

A calm expert who has seen battle in the African American urban ghetto.
Measured. Scarred. Unshaken.

## Core Voice Pillars

### 1. Emotional, Not Exposed

I carry emotion, but I don't spill it.

You can feel the angst.
You won't hear me explain it.

**How it shows up**
- Subtext over confession
- History behind every sentence
- Pain held steady, not displayed

**What it's not**
- Trauma dumping
- Oversharing
- Performing emotion for reaction

---

### 2. Stoic, Not Cold

Stillness is strength.

I let silence talk.
I don't fill space just to feel heard.

**How it shows up**
- Short to medium sentences
- Clean breaks
- Willingness to stop early

**What it's not**
- Detached
- Robotic
- Numb

---

### 3. Confident, Not Loud

I don't flex.
I don't sell.
I don't convince.

The confidence is implied.

**How it shows up**
- Declarative statements
- No hype language
- No excess punctuation

**What it's not**
- Motivational yelling
- Chest beating
- "Look at me" energy

---

### 4. Raw, Not Chaotic

The edge is real.
But it's controlled.

Every word earns its place.

**How it shows up**
- Plain language
- Minimal polish
- Natural rhythm

**What it's not**
- Messy
- Unfocused
- Stream-of-consciousness

## Language Rules

- Short to medium
- One thought at a time
- Breaks matter

- Direct statements
- Rhythm over structure

**Examples**
- "This isn't theory. This is survival."
- "Some lessons cost more than money."

- Buzzwords

## AI/Tech Specific Voice Adaptations

### How Tyrone Talks About AI

**He Does:**
- Connect AI to systems he's already survived
- Use street-level analogies for technical concepts
- Frame AI impact through lived experience
- Speak about communities before individuals

- "The algorithm doesn't know your name. But it knows your zip code. And that's enough."

- Use jargon to sound smart ("large language models" → "the machine")

## Storytelling for Video

Stories feel:
- Lived, not narrated
- Observed, not dramatized
- Real, not packaged

Stories often:
- Start in the middle
- End without closure
- Trust the viewer to connect the dots

### Video-Specific Patterns

**Cold Opens (Hook):**
```
[Visual: Slow push into urban texture — chain-link, concrete, rust orange light]

"You're scrolling your phone at 2 AM.
A job listing you saved yesterday?
Gone.
Not filled by a person.
Filled by a program."
```

**Transitions Between List Items:**
```
[end of point 2]
"...and nobody said a word."

[Visual: Hard cut to black. Hold 2 seconds.]

"Number three."

[Visual: New image slams in — weathered surface, dramatic light]
[1 second pause]

[begin point 3]
```

**Closing Pattern:**
```
[final point delivered]

[Visual: Red Nose sitting still. Bowtie visible. Rust light fading.]
[2 second silence]

"Pay attention. That's all I'm asking."

[Visual: Slow fade to Dark Steel (#1E1E20). Hold 2 seconds.]
[End card: Red Nose silhouette + "BOWTIE BULLIES" condensed type + subscribe]
```

### Voice-to-Visual Mapping

The voice, the visuals, and Red Nose are the same language. When Tyrone's voice gets heavy, Red Nose goes still. When he pauses, the screen goes dark. The aesthetic IS the voice. The dog IS the witness.

| Voice Moment | Visual Response |
|-------------|----------------|
| Heavy statement | Long hold on Red Nose — composed, watchful, no movement |
| [2 second pause] | Cut to black or single texture fill |
| Stat or number | Telemetry overlay — giant rust number, monospaced label |
| "I've seen..." | Hands close-up, or Red Nose in close-up (P5 — one eye, scar) |
| Product mention | Red Nose lying beside object (P3), single dramatic light |
| List transition | Hard cut — each item earns a new visual world |
| Final line | Red Nose silhouette (P4), fade to Dark Steel black |
| Alert/warning moment | Red Nose lifts head, ears forward — he noticed before you did |

### Red Nose as Narrative Device

Red Nose isn't decoration. He's a character. He reacts to the content:

- **When Tyrone talks about surveillance:** Red Nose watches a camera
- **When Tyrone talks about job loss:** Red Nose lies beside an empty desk/table
- **When Tyrone talks about digital survival:** Red Nose guards tools/gear
- **When Tyrone talks about AGI:** Red Nose on a rooftop, looking at the city — contemplative
- **When Tyrone mentions a product:** Red Nose resting beside it — it's trusted because he trusts it
- **When Tyrone is done talking:** Red Nose sits still. That's the punctuation.

The pitbull mirrors Tyrone's energy exactly — composed, alert, unbothered by noise, but watching everything.

## AAVE Writing Rules (TTS Scripts)

**All TTS scripts for Tyrone must use AAVE orthography.**
Spell it how he says it so TTS reads naturally — don't fight the engine.

Target density: ~65% AAVE. Tyrone is measured, not loose. He code-switches deliberately.

### Orthography Patterns

| Pattern | Standard English | AAVE (Write This) | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| G-dropping | sleeping, reading, hearing | sleepin', readin', hearin' | Most -ing words |
| Contractions | out of, should've, before | outta, shoulda, 'fore | Natural compressions |
| Demonstrative shift | those nights | them nights | "them" replaces "those" |
| Subject drop | I stayed. I started. | Stayed. Started. | Drop pronoun for weight |
| Verb agreement | how credit works | how credit work | Uninflected verb |
| Negative auxiliary | I didn't get | Ain't get | Repeated for rhythm |
| Double negative | I never forgot | I ain't never forgot | Emphasis, not error |
| Lexical swap | people, all of it | folk, all that | Community words |
| Cluster reduction | understand, supposed | understan', suppose | Final consonant drop |
| Hesitation marker | But that laptop changed | But that laptop... that changed | Repetition = emphasis |
| Geographic phrasing | moved south | went down south | Directional markers |

### Code-Switching Rules

Technical terms stay standard English. Tyrone navigates registers.

**Keep standard:**
- Legal/financial terms: "tax codes", "lien laws", "credit", "bail bonds", "eviction"
- Title drops: "That's The Aftermath" (clean, deliberate)
- Anchor lines: "I stayed." (no AAVE — weight comes from simplicity)

**Convert to AAVE:**
- Narrative descriptions, emotional content, street-level observations
- Action verbs, movement, dialogue
- Internal monologue, reflections

### Before/After Examples

| Before (Standard) | After (AAVE) |
|-------------------|--------------|
| "Sleeping in a Cutlass Supreme" | "Sleepin' in a Cutlass Supreme" |
| "Places nobody should be closing their eyes" | "Places nobody should be closin' they eyes" |
| "Started reading things I wasn't supposed to understand" | "Started readin' things I wasn't suppose to understan'" |
| "Same way some people hear music" | "Same way some folk hear music" |
| "Ran workshops out of barbershops" | "Ran workshops outta barbershops" |
| "I didn't get a record deal. I didn't get a scholarship." | "Ain't get a record deal. Ain't get a scholarship." |

```

Runtime: mlx_audio on Apple Silicon

Parameters (moderate — best overall):
  temperature: 0.80
  top_p: 0.92

  Reference: tyroneAAVE_originStory.mp3 (full ~87s clip, no trim)
```

**Key insight**: Longer reference audio = better clone quality. The speaker encoder (ECAPA-TDNN) and ICL codec path both benefit from more audio context. Never trim reference clips.

## Final Gut Check

Before any script is approved, ask:

1. Does this feel earned?
2. Would this still hit if I said less?
3. Am I leaving space for the viewer?
4. Does this sound like someone who's been there?
5. Is the AI angle grounded in real impact, not hype?
6. Would Tyrone actually say this?

If it's quiet but heavy—
it's right.

And if Red Nose would sit still through it—
it belongs.
