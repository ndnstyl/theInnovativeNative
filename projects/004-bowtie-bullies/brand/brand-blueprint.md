### The Mascot: Red Nose

An **American Red Nose Pitbull** — gentleman strategist in sleeveless black coveralls and black tactical boots. The face of a faceless channel.

The pitbull IS the brand because the pitbull IS the metaphor:
- **Judged by appearance before being known** — breed-specific legislation = racial profiling
- **Legislated against by systems that never met him** — banned breed lists written by people who've never owned one
- **Stronger than people expect** — and chose to be gentle
- **Loyal, protective, community-first** — the block's dog. Not the suburb's dog.
- **The BowTie Mark** — Chevrolet bowtie silhouette used as brand symbol on vehicles, environments, and channel branding. "I chose to be better than how you see me"

Red Nose is not a cartoon mascot. He's rendered in 2D cel-shaded style — cinematic, dramatic, with clean lines and intentional stylization. He sits in the same world as the content — dark steel, rust light, urban textures. He's not cute. He's composed. His energy is controlled intelligence — a gentleman strategist, not a beast.

### Fallout Raccoon Thumbnail Pattern Analysis → BowTie Bullies Adaptation

#### Fallout Raccoon's Proven Formula (440K avg across 9 tracked videos)

Based on Airtable data analysis of all 9 Fallout Raccoon videos:

**Pattern 1: The Raccoon Is Always Present**
Fallout Raccoon uses their raccoon mascot as the recurring visual anchor across thumbnails. The mascot creates instant brand recognition in browse. Viewers scrolling see the raccoon and know the channel before reading a word.

**Pattern 2: Dark/Dystopian Backgrounds**
Every thumbnail uses a dark, moody, post-apocalyptic background. Bunkers, ruins, warehouses, empty roads. The palette is desaturated, dark, with one or two accent colors (usually red/orange/yellow for text).

**Pattern 3: Bold Condensed Text — Fear/Urgency Language**
Text is ALWAYS bold, condensed, uppercase. Usually 3-6 words. Heavy use of red, yellow, or white text. Words that perform: "BLACKLIST", "SEIZE", "CRISIS", "LIED", "HIDE", "SURVIVE", "FORTRESS", "NUCLEAR".

**Pattern 4: Single Concept Per Thumbnail**
Never cluttered. One idea. One image. One text block. The raccoon + one background element + text = done.

**Pattern 5: Number Prominence**
Numbers ("5 Things", "3 Technologies", "7 Tools") are visually emphasized — larger font or different color from the rest of the text.

**Pattern 6: Lighting = Mood**
Dramatic single-source lighting. The subject (mascot or object) is lit while the background falls into shadow. Creates depth and draws the eye.

### Voice Identity: Tyrone

#### Name Protocol
Tyrone's crew never calls him by his actual name. He is addressed as:
- **"T"** — the default
- **"Unk"** — respect, elder status
- **"OG"** — earned authority

**NEVER "Tyrone."** If someone slips and uses his real name, they get the stare — the fiercest "if looks could kill" stare-down. No words. Just eyes. The kind of silence that makes the room recalibrate. It's happened. It doesn't happen twice.

In scripts: crew/community characters address him as "T", "Unk", or "OG" only. The rare slip is a dramatic beat — played for tension, not comedy.

**ElevenLabs Configuration:**
- Voice ID: `[USER TO PROVIDE — Tyrone's cloned/selected voice]`
- Stability: 0.60-0.65 (slightly lower = more natural variation)
- Similarity Boost: 0.75-0.80
- Style: 0.15 (subtle emotional variation)
- Speaker Boost: ON

**Delivery Rules:**
- 110-125 WPM (slower than average — weight in every word)
- 2-3 intentional silences per video (1.5-3 seconds each)
- No vocal fry. No uptalk. No hype inflection.
- Drops in volume = emphasis (not rises)
- Cadence: short sentence. pause. short sentence. longer sentence that lands. silence.

### FFMPEG Assembly Command Template (Long-Form)
```bash
ffmpeg -y \
  -i voiceover.mp3 \
  -i music.mp3 \
  -i clip1.mp4 -i clip2.mp4 -i clip3.mp4 ... \
  -filter_complex "
    [0:a]volume=1.0[vo];
    [1:a]volume=0.12[music];
    [vo][music]amix=inputs=2:duration=first[audio];
    [2:v]scale=1920:1080,setsar=1[v0];
    [3:v]scale=1920:1080,setsar=1[v1];
    ...
    [v0][v1]xfade=transition=fade:duration=0.5:offset=5[vt0];
    ...
    [final_raw]eq=contrast=1.15:brightness=-0.03:saturation=0.75,
    unsharp=5:5:0.5,
    noise=alls=20:allf=t+u,
    vignette=PI/4:1.2[final_v]
  " \
  -map "[final_v]" -map "[audio]" \
  -c:v libx264 -preset medium -crf 18 \
  -c:a aac -b:a 128k \
  -r 24 -pix_fmt yuv420p \
  -movflags +faststart \
  output.mp4
```
**Note:** The final filter chain applies the Override color grade: boosted contrast, reduced saturation to 75%, subtle sharpening, film grain noise, and vignette. Music ducked to 0.12 (barely felt, not heard).
