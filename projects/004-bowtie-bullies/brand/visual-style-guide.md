## Core Visual Identity

### Mood

Raw textures. Earth tones. Desaturated metals. Cold shadows.
Rust, grit, scars, contrast.

The world's fallen — this looks like it.

Think:
- Scratched metal on a project stairwell railing
- Cracked concrete on a block that never got repaved
- Weathered leather on a work belt that's seen 20 years
- Canvas with oil stains from a shop that stayed open when everything else closed

Keep color minimal. Let the weight of the image speak.

### The Urban Ghetto Lens

This isn't suburban bunker fantasy. This is the aesthetic of a man who grew up where infrastructure already failed. The visuals carry that history:

- **Textures from the block:** Chain-link fence shadows, bodega security cameras, project hallway fluorescents, cracked sidewalk weeds pushing through concrete
- **Tools of self-reliance:** Hands working, wrench turning, wire stripping, soldering iron glow, screen light on a face you never fully see
- **Surveillance state:** Corner store cameras, phone screens, algorithm visualizations, data streams — the digital version of being watched
- **Earned objects:** Beat-up journal on a nightstand, cash in an envelope, analog clock, hand-crank radio — things that work when the grid doesn't
- **Financial survival:** Cash being counted, envelope budgeting, ledger books, coins stacked, wallets worn thin, bills spread on a kitchen table
- **Food security:** Container gardens on fire escapes, seed packets, canning jars lined up, water filters, community garden plots between buildings, hands in soil
- **Shelter & energy:** Solar panels on urban rooftops, portable power stations, first aid kits laid open, hand tools on a workbench, boarded windows with purpose
- **Historical weight:** Crumbling stone columns, faded currency, empty government buildings, abandoned infrastructure — the visual language of what collapse looks like after

Every visual should feel like a field report from someone who's been surviving systems his whole life. The survival pillar visuals add: gardens growing from concrete, money being counted by hand, tools being used with purpose, and the quiet evidence of self-sufficiency.

### Color Rules
1. **Dark Steel is the default.** Everything lives on this.
2. **Rust Orange is your one accent.** Use it like you use your voice — sparingly, with weight.
3. **Bone White is text.** Clean against dark. Never pure `#FFFFFF`.
4. **No gradients** unless they're vignette shadows (earned darkness, not decoration).
5. **No bright colors.** If it would glow on a screen, it doesn't belong here.
6. **Earth Clay and Ash Gray** fill the space between. They're structural, not decorative.
7. **BowTie Crimson is reserved** for the Chevrolet BowTie Mark trim. Never used for text, backgrounds, or other accents. ≤5% of frame.

## Typography

### Primary Font — Headlines & Titles

**Font:** Bangers (Google Fonts)
- Bold, comic-inspired display font
- High impact at all sizes (thumbnails, overlays, intros, captions)
- ALL UPPERCASE for headlines
- Letter-spacing: +2-4% (intentional breathing room)

### Secondary Font — Body, Captions & Data

**Font:** Montserrat (Google Fonts)
- Clean geometric sans-serif
- Readable at all sizes — from thumbnails to lower thirds
- Used for: captions, data overlays, descriptions, lower thirds, subtitles
- Weight: 600-700 (SemiBold to Bold) for emphasis, 400 for body
- Sentence case or UPPERCASE

### Fallback Font — Data & Legacy

**Font:** Anton (Google Fonts)
- Condensed sans-serif fallback when Bangers is unavailable
- Numbers, stats, coordinates, timestamps
- Used where a narrower letterform is needed

### Typography Rules
1. **Headlines = UPPERCASE.** Always. No exceptions. Bangers.
2. **Body text = sentence case.** Montserrat. Clean and readable.
3. **Data = Montserrat or Anton fallback.** Numbers, stats, dates.
4. **Letter spacing is silence.** Space between letters = space between thoughts.
5. **Never decorative fonts.** No scripts, no serifs, no anything with personality beyond function.
6. **Intentional silence in spacing** — let the type breathe like the voice breathes.

### Size Hierarchy (Video Overlays)

| Element | Size | Font | Color |

### Thumbnail Rules

1. **Red Nose appears in EVERY thumbnail.** He IS the brand recognition. Like Fallout Raccoon's raccoon.
2. **3-5 words maximum.** Not a sentence. A punch.
3. **Heavy vignette edges.** The world falls off into darkness around Red Nose.
4. **Red Nose lit like spotlight.** One source of light on him. Everything else shadow.
5. **Coveralls are ALWAYS visible** — the visual signature. Even in silhouette, the coveralls read.
6. **NO human face. NO emoji. NO arrows. NO circles.** This isn't that channel.
7. **Color: Dark Steel bg + Bone White text + ONE Rust Orange accent word.**
8. **Numbers in Rust Orange.** When a title has a number, it's the accent.

## Motion + Editing Aesthetic

### Pace

Measured. Not frantic.
No jump-cuts just to cut.
Each cut earns its place.

Like the voice — if a shot is heavy, let it hold.

### Cinematic Rules

| Parameter | Value | Why |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| Frame Rate | 24fps (long-form), 30fps (shorts) | 24fps = cinema weight. 30fps = platform standard. |
| LUT/Grade | High contrast, desaturated, slightly gritty | Matches the rust/steel palette. No clean corporate look. |
| Film Grain | 15-25% | Texture. Truth. Not perfection. |
| Letterbox | Optional 2.39:1 for cinematic moments | Use on philosophical segments. Not on data/stats. |
| Aspect Ratio | 16:9 (long-form), 9:16 (shorts/reels) | Platform standard. |

### Color Grading Specs (DaVinci Resolve / FFMPEG)

```
Base Temperature: 4800K (slightly cool)
Saturation: -20% to -30% from natural
Contrast: +15% to +20%
Shadow Lift: Slight — shadows are DARK but not crushed
Highlight Roll-off: Soft — no blown whites
Midtone Shift: Slightly warm (toward rust/earth)
Film Grain: Fine 35mm, 15-25% opacity
Vignette: 15-25% edge darkening, oval, soft falloff
```

### FFMPEG Color Grade Filter

```bash
# Post-assembly color grade for BowTie Bullies aesthetic
-vf "
  eq=contrast=1.15:brightness=-0.03:saturation=0.75,
  unsharp=5:5:0.5,
  noise=alls=20:allf=t+u,
  vignette=PI/4:1.2
"
```

### Transition Rules

| Transition | When To Use | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Hard Cut | Between list items, data changes | Instant |
| Fade to Black | End of sections, before silence | 1.0-1.5s |
| Dissolve | Between related visuals (same topic) | 0.5-0.8s |
| Slow Push | Into close-up, into data, into object | 3-6s (Ken Burns) |
| **NEVER** | Wipes, swipes, zoom transitions, star wipes | — |

### Ken Burns (Static Images)

```
Zoom Range: 1.0x → 1.08x (barely perceptible — earned movement)
Duration: 5-8 seconds per image
Direction: Alternate zoom-in and slow pan
Ease: Ease-in-out (no sudden starts)
```

## Audio Aesthetic

### Background Music

Deep ambient. Weighted.
Not busy. Not upbeat. Not "royalty-free vibes."

| Mood | BPM | Style | When |
|------|-----|-------|------|
| Dark Ambient | 50-70 | Sub-bass, field recordings, industrial undertones | The Systems content |
| Sparse Drums | 70-85 | Minimal trap/boom-bap pattern, muted | When message lands |
| Melancholy Keys | 60-80 | Single piano notes, long decay, reverb | Job displacement, loss |
| Tension | 75-95 | Low pulsing synth, distant alarm tones | Surveillance, crisis |
| Contemplative | 45-65 | Ambient pads, no rhythm, space | AGI/ASI philosophy |

### Audio Mix Rules

```
VO Level: -12 to -14 dB (primary — always audible)
Music Level: -26 to -32 dB (background — felt, not heard)
Silence Level: -inf (intentional — 1.5 to 3 seconds)
Ambient SFX: -22 to -28 dB (city sounds, rain, hum — sparse)
```

### Intentional Silence

Silence is a beat, not an accident.

- After a heavy statement: **2 seconds** of nothing
- Between list items: **1.5 seconds**
- Before the close: **2-3 seconds**
- Music dips to -40dB during silence (nearly inaudible hum)

### Audio Post-Processing (VO)

```
EQ: Roll off below 80Hz (remove room rumble)
Compression: Gentle (3:1 ratio, slow attack)
De-ess: Light (preserve character)
Reverb: NONE (dry. Present. In the room with you.)
Normalization: -14 LUFS (YouTube standard)
```

## Persona Presentation (Faceless + Red Nose Mascot)

No human face shown. Presence comes from **Tyrone's voice, Red Nose's visual presence, and the world they inhabit.**

### The Two Presences

**Red Nose (Visual):** The pitbull IS the face of the channel. He appears in thumbnails, intros, outros, and key moments in video. He's rendered in 2D cel-shaded style — cinematic, dramatic, with clean lines — never photorealistic, never cartoonish. He reacts to the content. When Tyrone talks about surveillance, Red Nose looks at a camera. When Tyrone is contemplative, Red Nose sits still on a rooftop.

**Tyrone (Audio):** The voice. Never seen. But his presence is felt through POV camera angles (his eyes) and hands (his actions). Tyrone is the narrator. Red Nose is the witness.

### POV Angles (Tyrone's Eyes)

- **Hands working:** Close-up on hands opening a Faraday bag, writing in a journal, turning an analog dial, holding a phone showing data. Red Nose visible in background, resting nearby.
- **Over-the-shoulder:** Looking at a screen, looking at a city street, looking at surveillance footage
- **POV walk:** Slow walk through urban environment (generated via Kie.AI/Nano Banana)
- **Object reveals:** Slow push into an object on a surface — radio, journal, tool, phone. Red Nose's paw or ear at edge of frame.

### What You See / What You Don't

| Show | Don't Show |
|------|-----------|
| Red Nose (full, partial, silhouette) | Human faces |
| Hands (close-up, working) | Full human body |
| Red Nose's coveralls + boots (always) | Red Nose without coveralls |
| Red Nose composed, alert, dignified | Red Nose aggressive, barking, teeth showing |
| Urban textures (concrete, metal, chain-link) | Suburban yards, dog parks, grass |
| Objects lit dramatically | Clean, bright, corporate spaces |
| Screens with data/code | Unrelated stock footage |
| Tools and gear beside Red Nose | Products displayed like QVC |

## Vehicle Identity

Vehicles in the BowTie Bullies world are always blacked-out, always understated.

```
Paint:       Matte or satin black only — no color, no chrome
Wheels:      Blacked-out — no chrome, no polished aluminum
Trim:        All trim blacked out (badges, handles, mirrors)
Accents:     Deep crimson red (#8B0000) only:
             - Brake calipers (visible through wheels)
             - Thin pinstripe along body line
             - BowTie Mark emblem on grille (matte black + crimson trim)
Windows:     Dark tint
```

## Visual Quality Checklist

Before any visual ships:

- [ ] **Palette check:** Only Dark Steel, Rust, Clay, Ash, Bone White visible?
- [ ] **No bright colors?** No neon, no saturation, no blue, no green (unless Moss accent)?
- [ ] **Film grain present?** 15-25% opacity, fine texture?
- [ ] **High contrast?** Shadows are dark, highlights are controlled?
- [ ] **Typography correct?** Headlines = condensed sans uppercase, body = monospaced?
- [ ] **Vignette applied?** Edges darker than center?
- [ ] **Faceless?** No human faces in frame?
- [ ] **Red Nose consistent?** Coat color, nose, eyes, ears, scar, coveralls, boots match reference?
- [ ] **Coveralls visible?** Coveralls visible on Red Nose, even in silhouette?
- [ ] **Urban texture present?** Does this feel like it came from the block?
- [ ] **One focal point?** Not cluttered. One idea per frame.
- [ ] **Would Tyrone approve?** Restrained. Earned. Not flashy.
- [ ] **Would Red Nose approve?** Composed. Dignified. Not cute. Not aggressive.
