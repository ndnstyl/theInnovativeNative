## TEXT FORMATTING RULES FOR ELEVENLABS

| Technique | Effect | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|

| CAPS | Word emphasis | "ZERO business equity" |

*The quietest section. Tyrone gets quieter, not louder, when it matters. The listener leans in.*

### SCENE 1 — MAMA LOST THE HOUSE
**Delivery:** Near-whisper. A fact being placed on the table, not a story being told.
**Speed:** 0.85 (slowest speed — each syllable carries weight)
**Stability:** 0.42 (raw, slightly unsteady — memory hurts)
**Style:** 0.28 (pulled way back)

```
Mama lost the house in oh-three.
```

**Notes:** Seven words. Let them land. The pause after is as important as the line itself.

### SCENE 2 — THE LETTER
**Delivery:** Building from the opening. Still quiet but more words — the story is starting to move. "I stayed." is the hardest line. Two words, completely alone.

**Style:** 0.30

Wasn't no warnin'. Just a letter. Then a lock on the door. She went down south. Family in Louisiana said they had room. I stayed.

**Notes:** "I stayed." — Full stop. Isolation. This is not a boast. It's a fact that still costs him to say.

## RECORDING NOTES

1. **Scene 46 splits into two VO files** — the 2-second pause between "AI just made it urgent for everybody else." and "Each one teach one." is added in post-production, not TTS
2. **All `[pause]` markers in the screenplay are timeline cues** — they're stripped before sending to ElevenLabs and added back during FFMPEG assembly
3. **AAVE orthography is the text** — don't convert to standard English. ElevenLabs reads "ain't" and "folk" correctly with this voice clone
4. **Similarity boost stays at 0.75 for all scenes** — keeps the voice consistent while other settings create emotional range
5. **Speaker boost stays ON for all scenes** — non-negotiable for voice consistency
6. **Act 1 volume drops are CRITICAL** — scenes 10-11 should be noticeably quieter than scenes 1-9. The listener leans in.
7. **Quote card scenes (24, 27, 29, 33, 44, 47) have NO voiceover** — text on screen only
